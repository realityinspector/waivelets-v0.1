What We Found

When you embed sentences with a neural network and analyze how the 384-dimensional trajectory unfolds over time, four distinct structural modes emerge. These are not genres. They describe how meaning moves — and they cut across genre boundaries in historically coherent ways.

Convergent
Narrows to few attractor basins and holds. High coherence, concentrated semantic range. Liturgical, aphoristic, legalistic, or meditative structure.
Augustine, Bhagavad Gita, Dickinson, Locke, KJV Bible
Contemplative
Thinks about thinking. High interiority, low scene narration. Self-aware analysis — text that reflects on its own process.
Darwin, Dickens (Pickwick), Shakespeare (Tempest), Swift, Russell
Discursive
The wide river. Moderate pace, many attractor basins visited, balanced semantic domains. Sustained shaped discourse — narrative, argument, or epic.
Doyle, Wilde, Wells, Twain, Austen, Dostoevsky, Plato, Dante
Dialectical
Rapid oscillation between registers. Dramatic mode-switching, systematic contrast. Each sentence pushes away from the last.
Shakespeare (plays), Darwin (Origin), Melville, Dickens, Tao Te Ching
Key Insight

These modes are not genres. Shakespeare and Darwin share a mode (dialectical) because they both do rapid systematic contrast between registers. The Bible and a graph database textbook share a mode (convergent) because they both narrow to a few semantic basins and repeat. Plato and Austen share a mode (discursive) because they're both doing sustained shaped discourse. The modes capture what texts DO, not what they're called.

How It Works

Three steps. No LLM inference. No fine-tuning. Just linear algebra on embeddings.

1. Embed → 2. Project → 3. Measure

Embed: Convert each sentence to a 384-dimensional vector using a small sentence transformer (MiniLM, 22M params).
Project: Multiply by a precomputed 384×26 eigenbasis matrix (37KB). This projects each sentence onto 26 attractor coordinates.
Measure: Compute smoothness (cosine similarity between consecutive projections), oscillation (variance of smoothness), cluster activations (4 semantic domains), and basin entropy. Total: 7 numbers.

The Discovery Pipeline

The eigenbasis was found through continuous wavelet transforms on the embedding sequences, building 384×384 correlation matrices across dimensions, and extracting topological invariants. This is the expensive part (~minutes per text). But once the eigenbasis is computed, it's reusable — the wavelets were the telescope, the eigenbasis is the map. You don't need the telescope once you have the map.

Ultra-Low Compute

For pre-embedded corpora, classification is 10,000+ documents per second on a single CPU core. The entire classifier — eigenbasis + cluster map + mode centroids — fits in 38KB. This could run on a microcontroller. The embedding step (22M param model) is the only meaningful cost, and it's a one-time operation per text.

Why This Matters

The fingerprint opens several doors that are hard to open with traditional NLP.

Few-Shot Style Transfer by Dynamics

Instead of selecting few-shot examples by topic ("find examples about databases"), select by dynamical fingerprint ("find examples that unfold like a textbook"). This gives you two-axis control: WHAT to say × HOW to unfold it.

AI Text Detection

If LLM-generated text has different attractor dynamics than human text — plausible, since LLMs lack multi-scale self-similarity — the fingerprint could detect it at near-zero cost.

Cultural & Persona Fingerprints

A corpus's mode distribution characterizes how that body of text structures meaning. Different literary traditions, authors, and LLM personas would have different distributions. The deviation from expected is a measure of authenticity.

Ultra-Low Compute Structural Analysis

The 38KB classifier could run on embedded systems, edge devices, or in browser. Pre-embedded text can be classified at 10,000 docs/second. This enables structural text analysis at scales and costs that are impractical for any LLM-based approach.

Methodology & References

The discovery chain: wavelets → correlation → topology → attractors → modes.

Embedding model: all-MiniLM-L6-v2 (22M params, 384 dims). Reimers & Gurevych, 2019.

Wavelet transform: Continuous Wavelet Transform with Morlet wavelet, validated across 10 wavelet families. Torrence & Compo, 1998.

Topological analysis: Graph Laplacian spectrum, Betti numbers, fractal exponents via log-log regression. Adapted from IMT topology methods.

Attractor analysis: Hebbian correlation → eigendecomposition → Hopfield-like attractor landscape with 26 effective modes. Ramsauer et al., 2021 (Modern Hopfield Networks).

Autoresearch: 75-iteration Karpathy-style configuration search across wavelets, thresholds, shuffle methods, and correlation metrics.

Validation: 100-text Project Gutenberg corpus spanning 8 genres, 2,500 years. Four dynamical modes discovered via unsupervised clustering.
